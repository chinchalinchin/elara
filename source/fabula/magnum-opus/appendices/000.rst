.. ## COLOR & STYLE
..
..      **Guidelines**
..
..      - Use the principles of Material UI when making updates to the styles.
..      - Use the principles of Color Theory and Color Wheels when selecting new colors.
..      - Make sure the mermaid graphs are well-commented, but on the flip side, don't be too verbose.
..
..      **Gradient**
..
..      - Color gradient is used to visualize the descent from the earlier generations (Darker) to the later generations (Lighter).
..      - Color gradient applies to all shades except Special and External, which have a only single shade.
..
..      Think of color space as denoting durations of time. Shades are supposed to get lighter as time goes on. When a branch diverges from another branch, the color should change naturally, moving continuously through color space.

.. ## NODES
..
..      **Shapes**
..
..      - Circle (): Only Killiaen van Rensselaer, the 1st Patron
..      - American Patroons {{ }}: Hexagons.
..      - Dutch Patroons ([]): Stadium-shaped Node
..      - Generic Male of Male Descent []: Rectangles
..      - Generic Female of Male Descent {}: Diamonds
..      - Generic Male of Female Descent [/\]: Inverse Trapezoid
..      - Generic Female of Female Descent [\/]: Trapezoid
..      - External Node: Plain Rectangle
..      - Framed Rectangle [[]]: Exit Points of Branches.
..      - Framed Circles ((())): Entry through Branches
..
..      Patroon shapes take precedence. American Patroonship takes precedence over Dutch Patroonship.
..
..      When a branch enters another branch, the shade of the entrypoint should *always* match the shade of the branch gradient from which they exited.

.. ## RELATIONS
.. 
..      **Marriage**
..
..      - The relations between husbands and wives should include the year they were married (if they were married), prefixed with "m. <year>". 
..      - Dotted lines indicate the marriage of cousins.
..      - Thick lines indicates the marriage of a daughter to an external family. Only the last name of the external connection should be listed.
..      
..      **Extinction**
..
..      - The relation between an ancestor and their descendant who had no issue should be included. Lines that end with a "x" denote this relationship.
..      - The "x" should terminate on the final descendant of this branch. Do not add an extra node for the exit.
..      - Once a branch reaches this point, it does not need to factor into further searches.
..      - Once a branch is terminated, this should retroactively turn the prior connections from (-->) into dots (--o), so the lines that end can be tracked down the diagram. The rule should apply up to the node where the ancestor rejoins the (-->) flow.

The Prophecy (1656 - 1674)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

The Manor's survival under English rule was secured not by the competence of Ryckert, the acting Director, but by the prophetic delusions of the family's prodigal son, **Rev. Nicolaes van Rensselaer**. Years prior, Nicolaes had approached the exiled **King Charles II** in Brussels, claiming to have a vision of the King's restoration to the throne. When the prophecy came true in 1660, the superstitious Monarch granted Nicolaes his lifelong favor (and a gold snuffbox). In 1674, facing the confiscation of their Dutch patent by the English Crown, the family installed Nicolaes as Director. The gamble worked. The "*mad prophet*" arrived with a warrant from the Duke of York, rendering the estate untouchable by local magistrates who dared not cross the King's favorite mystic.

The Schuyler Alliance (1783-1787)
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

In a dynastic maneuver, the Van Rensselaer and Schuyler families secured their dominance in post-Revolutionary New York through a "double exchange" of siblings. **Stephen van Rensselaer III** (the 8th Patroon) and his sister Elizabeth married the children of **General Philip Schuyler**, Margarita and **John Bradstreet Schuyler**. Because General Schuyler’s wife was herself a Van Rensselaer (from the Plebian branch), this alliance grafted the divergent Elder and Plebian branches back together. The resulting children were "double first cousins," sharing all four grandparents.

Genealogy

.. topic:: Terminology

    - Dutch Branch (Lords of Cralo): Male branch that remained in Holland.
    - Elder Branch (Lords of the Manor): Male branch that relocated to America.
        - Patrician Branch (Lords of the Manor): Male branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Equestrian Branch (Non-inheriting): Male branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Trophonian Branch (Non-inheriting): Female branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Dodonic Branch (Non-inheriting): Female branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Sibylline Branch (Non-inherting): Female branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Chthonic Branch (Non-inheriting): Female branch of the Elder Branch.
        - Cumaean Branch (Non-inheriting): Female branch of the Patrician Branch.
        - Clarian Branch (Non-inheriting): Female branch of the Patrician Branch.
    - Plebian Branch (Lords of Claverack): Male branch that started in America.
        - Mithraic Branch (Non-inheriting): Male branch of the Plebian branch.
        - Eleusinian Branch (Non-inheriting): Male branch of the Plebian Branch.
