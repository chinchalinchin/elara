..      - Use the principles of Material UI when making updates to the styles.
..
.. COLOR
..
..      - Primary (Deep Purple): Represents the Patroon Lineage (Lords of the Manor). 
..      - Secondary (Amber): Represents Family Members (Wives, siblings, younger branches).
..      - Tertiary (Teal): Represents Special Individuals (e.g., Solomon van Rensselaer). 
.. 
.. CONSTRAINTS
..
..      - Patroons are identified by the color scheme and a hexagonal shape.
..      - Color gradient is used to visualize the descent of the title from the earlier generations (Darker) to the later generations (Lighter).
..      - Color gradient only applies to Primary and Secondary schemes. Tertiary only has a single shade.
..      - Never remove a node from the tree.
..      - Generations should always be aligned to descending rows.
..      - Nodes should only contain: name, birth - death dates, and possibly "n-th Patroon"
..      - If birth and death years are unknown, include approximationes with a "?" to denote uncertainty.
..      - The relations between husbands and wives should include the year they were married (if they were married). If the date is unknown, use a "?" to denote uncertainty.
