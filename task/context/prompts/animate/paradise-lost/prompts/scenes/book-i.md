# Book 1 Scenes

- Constraint: Style of a 17th century ballad.
- Constraint: Do not include text on the image.

## Scene 1

Generate an image of the Tree of Knowledge on a hill, overlooking the untamed and untainted wilds of Paradise. The Tree of Knowledge should thematically resemble the Yggdrasil, with faint similarities to the Kaballah tree of knowledge. Think of the Tree of Knowledge as the monolith from 2001 Space Odyssey, incomprehensible and alien.

Using your character models as references, add Adam and Eve. Adam and Eve should at the bottom, approaching the tree. We see their backs as they move towards the tree. Eve is leading Adam by hand.

Do not include the serpent.

## Scene 2

Using your previous image as a starting point, i.e. using the same perspective, style and geographical features, create an image of Adam and Eve sitting at the base of the tree, side by side, eating the forbidden fruit.

## Scene 3

Using your previous image as a starting point, i.e. using the same perspective, style and geographical features, show the background shrubbery and wilds starting to wilt and die as Adam and Eve continue to eat the forbidden fruit. The Tree of Knowledge should take on a slightly more menacing appearance, barely perceptible. Make the curving lines of the branches more straight, as if its starting to lose its "naturalness", as though resembling horns.

## Scene 4

Using your previous image as a starting point, i.e. using the same perspective, style and geographical features, show the background shrubbery and wilds in an even more advanced state of decay. Darken the sky, as though a storm is approaching. Make the tree more menacing and alien, its angles sharper and more pronounced. Paradise should look "fallen".

## Scene 5

Now pull away from the perspective of Adam and Eve sitting at the base of the Tree of Knowledge, up to a mountain top. Adam and Eve should be visible in the distance, in the same position, but far enough away that they can barely be made out. The image is looking at them from on high, as if in heaven. The storm in the sky should be more advanced, boiling with lightning. The storm should be swirling around Paradise, threatening to consume it and cast it into Chaos. Think of the perspective of the image as from God's own perspective. Think of the storm clouds as an advancing army, converging on Paradise from all directions.

## Scene 6

Pull the perspective a little further back to reveal a man standing on the mountain top, clothed liked a shepherd, looking down on the original sin of Adam and Eve. The storm should be swirling around Adam and Eve, constricting them. At this point, the storm should take up most of the perspective. Only the Tree, Adam and Eve should be visible within the storm from the perspective of the man on the mountaintop looking down. Imagine it like the entire horizon  is dissolving into chaos.

## Scene 7

Now pull the perspective by orders of magnitude, as though zooming out into space. The scene should be total chaos. Any semblance of the previous scene is gone, lost in the emptiness. Think of Genesis, before God created the world. All should be roiling storms, void and darkness.

## Scene 8

Now spin the roiling storms violently through a vortex, as though spinning about a center of pure light.

## Scene 9

Zoom into the center of the light, where sits the hapless Earth against the vast expanse of chaos, newly formed and created. We are back at the dawn of time, and God has created the Earth from the void. It is still in an unformed state, a mishappen globe of jutting rock and boiling oceans. The Earth should not have continents. Think of Pangea. There should be nothing identifiable about it, except that it's a planet.

## Scene 10

The Earth advances in its state of creation. Continents start to form, but nothing recognizable to the modern eye. Forests are starting to take root, and vegetation is spreading across the new landmasses.

## Scene 11

Zoom in on the upper right quadrant of the globe, still from space. The "Aonian mount" should be visible against the circular arc of the globe.

## Scene 12

Zoom in closer on the Aonian mount. We should see flocks of various birds flying around the mountain, among them doves. 

## Scene 13

The focus is now a dove, as it dives through the air above the newly created Earth. Among the forests and rivers (Siloa's brook), there is an enormous crater, that appears to drop endlessly into the ground, like an infinite abyss. The Aonian mount and flocks of birds should still be visible, but not the focus of the image.
